About Me

I am a PhD student in developmental and educational psychology at the Institute of Psychology, Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS). My research focuses on children's cognitive development and social behavior, with a particular emphasis on interpersonal interaction and joint attention in children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). I am broadly interested in how children understand, respond to, and engage with others in social contexts.

Research Interests

  • Children’s cognitive development and social decision-making
  • Interpersonal interaction and cooperation in childhood
  • Joint attention and joint action, especially in children with ASD
  • Third-party intervention, fairness, and social norms in development

  • Peng, Y., Tang, J., & Li, H. (2021). Good deeds could come from frustrated individuals. Frontiers in Psychology, 12, 631711. https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.631711
